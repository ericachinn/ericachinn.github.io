How every Boeing 737 MAX was grounded in five days

Background

Link to original story

This is a story by Pablo Robles from the SCMP. Published on 1 April, the story is about how the plane model Boeing 737 Max was grounded by all countries after two crashes of said model in five months. The author illustrated why, when, and where the aircrafts were grounded by utilising various visulisations.

In this analysis, I would like to focus on the timeline the author used to depict the chronology.

Pros

  1. Mobile friendly

    2. First, the timeline is easily readable on both computer screens and phone screens. Second, the timeline is vertical, which is more mobile-friendly than a horizontal timeline because people are used to scrolling down their phones instead of scrolling sideways.

  2. Shows clear chronological order of the days

    3. The timeline shows clearly the day and time of the events, with exact time.

Cons

  1. Takes too long to scroll

    2. The timeline, in my opinion, requires too much scrolling to inform readers the chronology of the grounding of flights.

My take

I would like to keep the pros which I have mentioned above:

  • Mobile friendly
  • Shows clear chronological order of the days

  • Takes too long to scroll
